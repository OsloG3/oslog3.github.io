Minor suit raise

Inverted minors

1♣-2♣ and 1♦-2♦ show an invitational or better raise without a 4 card or longer major.
Inverted minors continuations:

  • New suits without jumps show stoppers, and are bid up the line.
  • Jumps to new suits show shortness.
  • Bidding NTs indicates that we think we have all suits stopped.

Bidding 2nt or 3 of the opened minors are always not forcing. By responder it shows he only had an invitational raise, and by opener it shows he would not accept an invite.

1m - 3m show a less than invitational raise and deny a side 4 card major. The hand should be strong enough to want to be in game opposite 18-19 balanced.

Major suit raises

Simples raise

1M-2M shows at least 3 card support and 5-10 points.
Opener can bid a new suit to show values in the bid suit and interest in game opposite a maximum (8-10 points)

Mixed raise

1M-3M shows 4 card support, 6-9 points and not a flat hand (4333 or a 4432 with a two honors in the doubleton)

Maas Raise

1M-2nt shows invitational or minimum gf(10-14 points) values 3 or more cards support.
Opener can bid 3M with a minimum to reject the invite or 4M if accepts the invite with no hope of slam.

2/1 delayed raise

When partner opens a major and we have 14/15+ points and a fit we start by bidding a new suit.

Other responses to 1 level openings

  • New suits at the 1 level show 4 or more cards and are forcing 1 round.
  • New suits at the 2 level without jumps show a gf hand two over one.
  • Single jumps to a new suit show 6+ cards in the bid suit and are invitational. They deny side 4 card majors and support for partner's suit
  • Double jumps to new suits are splinters.
  • 1m - 1nt shows 5-10 points and no suit biddable at the 1 level
  • 1m - 2nt is invitation balanced without 4 card majors
  • 1M-1nt shows 5-11 points without support for the opened majors, 1♥-1nt also denies 4 or more spades.

Bidding something different of the 2♦ waiting bid over the 2 club opener usually takes a lot of bidding space and makes it very difficult to fully describe the hand by the opener. Therefore we must be careful if we skip the relay and make it only with hands with good suits. If our suit is not good enough we should just bid the relay.

Weak 2 openings continuations

  • 3 level raise: preemptive raise with 3 card support
  • 4 level raise: To play, preemptive or with values
  • 2 level new suit: natural forcing 1 round
  • 3 level new suit: natural gf
  • Jump to new suits(below game): splinter with slam interest
  • 2nt: feature ask

2x - 2nt feature ask

  • 3x minimum
  • 3 new suit: maximum with values in the bid suit
  • 3nt: maximum with no values in the side suits
  • New suit 4rth level: Shortness on the bid suit

Fourth suit gf

When opener bids a second suit at the 2 or higher level then bidding the remaining unbid suit by the responder is game forcing and asks for more information about the opener’s hand.

1♥ - 1♠
2♣ -?

1♦ - 1♠
2♥ -?
…

Bidding the fourth suit (the last remaining unbid suit) is artificial and game forcing. It asks if opener has 3 card support for responder’s suit or a stopper in the fourth suit.
With no 3 card support nor a stopper in the fourth suit and no other descriptive bid to make opener can rebid his opening suit: waiting and showing no better bid.

Two suited overcalls

Unusual two no trumps:
(1x) - 2nt shows at least 5/5 in the lowest ranking unbid suits.
Micheal cuebid:
(1m) - 2m shows at least a 5/5 in the majors

(1M) - 2M shows at least 5 cards in the unbid major and 5 cards in one of the two minors. Partner can use 2nt(or 4nt) to ask which is the minor

After we overcall at the 1 or 2 level without jump

  • New suits at the 1 level are forcing for 1 round
  • New suits at the 2 level are not forcing but show a constructive or invitational hand
  • New suits at the 3 level are gf both with and without a jump
  • 1nt/2nt are both natural showing a stopper in the opponents’ suit. 1nt shows 8-11 points, 2nt after a 1 level overcall shows 12-14 points and 2nt after a 2 level overcall show 9-11 points.
  • Cuebidding opponents' suit without jump shows either an invitational or better support or a balanced hand with enough strength to play game but no stopper in opponents suit.
  • Jump cuebidding shows a mixed raise (4card support 6-9hcp and some distribution)
