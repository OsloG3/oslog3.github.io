Why this system

System design

The heart of the system is the 1♣ opening showing 4+♠ 9+hcp. With the boss suit we don't mind giving the opponents space to overcall as we are likely to buy the hands in spades and appreceate any information the opponents give us. The lower range (9+) for the opening is managable in most cases as having the boss suit means that we have more space to sort out ranges in competition, and at the same time we are starting at 1♣ in constructive auctions.
The 1♥ is undoubtedly high for a strong opening, but if opponents have an overcall of our strong opening it doesn't matter if we oppened 1♣ or 1♥, but it does matter that we have restricted our shapes. If opponents don't overcall then we might be behind something like a strong club.
The 1♠ opening is in between ea wak no trump opening and a precision 1♦. It suffers from some of the same problems as the precision 1♦ not promising any suit, but, similarly to 1♥, we have fewer possible shapes and if opponents overcall it doesn't matter that we started the bidding higher.
The 2♣ opening is just a very good opening. Denying a 4 card major it removes most of the problems of the precision 2♣(both the 5+ and the 6+♣ versions).
The 2♥ opening was choosen to provide an opening bid to 4♠-5♥ 9-10 hcp hands that are otherwise the only unbalanced hand with 4+♠ 9+hcp to not have an opening bid, but this is not required and 3 weak twos or any other preemptive schema is fine
